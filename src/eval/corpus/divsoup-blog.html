Reading in Three Dimensions
How spatial layout changes long-form comprehension.
Introduction Method Results References
Introduction
Virtual reality dissolves the page boundary that has framed reading for five centuries. Instead of a fixed rectangle, text can wrap around the reader and float at a comfortable arm's length.
This post reports a study of comprehension when the same document is presented flat versus curved at a 1.2 metre viewing distance.
Method
Twenty-four participants read four expository passages in a headset. Each passage subtended about thirty degrees of horizontal angle.
We measured reading speed, comfort, and a ten-item recall quiz.
The curved-panel condition at 1.2 m.
Results
Curved panels produced a small but reliable gain in comfort with no cost to recall. Reading speed was indistinguishable between conditions.
Comfort improved by 0.6 points on a 7-point scale.
Recall accuracy was 82% flat versus 84% curved.
Reading speed hovered near 240 words per minute in both.
