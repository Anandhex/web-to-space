Reading in Three Dimensions

How spatial layout changes long-form comprehension.

Introduction Method Results References

Introduction

Virtual reality dissolves the page boundary that has framed reading for five centuries. Instead of a fixed rectangle, text can wrap around the reader, float at a comfortable arm's length, and re-flow as the head turns.

This article reports a controlled study of comprehension when the same document is presented flat versus curved at a 1.2 metre viewing distance.

Method

Twenty-four participants read four expository passages in a Meta Quest 3. Each passage subtended roughly thirty degrees of horizontal visual angle.

We measured reading speed, self-reported comfort, and a ten-item recall quiz administered immediately after each passage.

Results

Curved panels produced a small but reliable gain in comfort with no cost to recall. Reading speed was statistically indistinguishable between conditions.

  • Comfort improved by 0.6 points on a 7-point scale.
  • Recall accuracy was 82% flat versus 84% curved.
  • Reading speed hovered near 240 words per minute in both.
